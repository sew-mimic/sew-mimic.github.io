Abstract

Popular forward / inverse kinematics solvers such as iterative jacobian, Mink, and Operation Space Controller offer generalized frameworks to solve for desired cartesian space translation and rotation for any robot arms with jacobian matrix available. These methods however lack in 1) computation speed, 2) solution reliability, and 3) pose matching accuracy.

In the context of teleoperation of bi-manual humanoid robot arms, the concept of cartesian space distance matching between the operator hands and robot end-effectors (EE) doesn't consider 1) robot arms and human arms have different sizes and limits for reach, 2) the operator's deliberate choice of elbow placement, and that 3) human body motion perception of translation and rotation are largely relative (we rely more on references and fast feedback from eyes), making direct hand-EE mapping less intuitive.

Our method proposes a fast (≥ 3000 Hz on NumPy), closed-form, geometric mapping between the robot arm configuration and human body poses using the Shoulder-Elbow-Wrist (SEW) positions. This method is suitable for most popular 7-degrees-of-freedom robot arms such as Kinova Gen3, KUKA IIWA, and most humanoid robots such as G1, GR1, and RBY1. It is also agnostic to the choice of input sensor - Meta Quest 3, MoCap cameras, or even standard webcams.
